"Exploring Yala's Natural Splendor"


Welcome to Yala National Park


Welcome to the magnificent Yala National Park, a gem among Sri Lanka's wildlife preserves. Yala, which is tucked away in the southeast of the island, is well known for its diverse range of wildlife and striking scenery.


Yala National Park is located in Sri Lanka's Southern and Uva provinces. With a vast expanse of roughly 978 square kilometers, it is the most visited national park in the nation and a sanctuary for those who love the outdoors.


Wildlife and Biodiversity:


As you step into Yala's realm, be prepared to witness an enchanting array of wildlife. Several of Sri Lanka's most recognizable animals can be found in the park, such as the shy leopard, the stately elephant, and the amusing sloth bear. The park's colorful tapestry is enhanced by the melodic calls of various bird species that fill the air. Yala's varied vegetation blends in well with its wildlife, resulting in a pleasing ecosystem that arouses the senses. Prepare yourself for an immersive journey that will bring you closer to the beating heart of the natural world at every turn.



Plan Your Visit

For those planning to explore Yala, here's what you need to know.

Entry Fees:


  • Adults (Foreigners): $25 per person
  • Children (5-12 years): $15 per child
  • Adults (Locals): LKR 2000 per person
  • Children (Locals): LKR 1000 per child
  • Discounts: Students and senior citizens enjoy a 20% discount on entry fees.
  • Modes of Payment: Cash is preferred. Credit cards are accepted at the main entrance. Online payments are not available.

Opening Hours:


  • Regular Hours: 6:00 AM to 6:00 PM
  • Seasonal Variations: The park may close earlier during the monsoon season. Check for updates.
  • Entry/Exit Times (Zones): Different zones may have specific entry and exit times. Check at the time of entry.

Permits:


  • Permit Process: Permits can be obtained at the park entrance.
  • Advance Booking: Permits for entry can be booked in advance online or obtained on-site.
  • Special Permits: Special permits are required for camping and professional photography.

Tips for a Successful Visit:


  • Best Times to Visit: Early morning and late afternoon for optimal wildlife sightings.
  • Clothing and Footwear: Wear neutral-colored clothing and comfortable, closed shoes.
  • Hydration: Carry sufficient water, especially during hot seasons.
  • Essentials: Sunscreen, insect repellent, and a hat are essential for a comfortable experience.

Recommended Activities:


  • Guided Safaris: Explore the park with experienced guides.
  • Nature Walks: Take a leisurely stroll on designated trails.
  • Bird Watching: Yala is a birdwatcher's paradise; don't forget your binoculars.
  • Local Guides: Enhance your experience by hiring knowledgeable local guides.

Guidelines for Wildlife Conservation and Responsible Tourism:


  • Respect Wildlife: Maintain a safe distance; do not feed or disturb animals.
  • Responsible Behavior: Follow designated trails, avoid littering, and adhere to park rules.
  • Conservation Contribution: Learn about the park's conservation efforts and consider making a donation..
